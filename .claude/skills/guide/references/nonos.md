# No-Nos: Anti-Patterns to Kill on Sight

These patterns make writing feel artificial, corporate, or lazy. Remove them ruthlessly.

## AI Slop

The telltale signs that a machine wrote this and no human bothered to edit it.

**Throat-clearing openers:**
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "In the ever-evolving landscape of..."
- "It's important to note that..."
- "It's worth mentioning that..."
- "As we all know..."
- "In this comprehensive guide..."

**Filler transitions:**
- Starting every paragraph with "Additionally," "Furthermore," "Moreover,"
- "That being said..."
- "With that in mind..."
- "Moving on to..."
- "Let's delve into..." / "Let's explore..."
- "Without further ado..."

**Empty conclusions:**
- "In conclusion..."
- "To sum up..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "In summary, we've covered..."
- Restating every point that was just made

**Structural tells:**
- Exactly 3 or 5 items in every list with suspiciously parallel construction
- Every section the same length
- A summary paragraph that restates the introduction
- Headers that are questions followed by answers that begin "Great question!"

**Weasel phrases:**
- "It should be noted that..."
- "One might argue that..."
- "It goes without saying..."
- "Needless to say..." (then why are you saying it?)
- "This is a game-changer"
- "This powerful tool"

## Corporate Speak

Words that sound important and mean nothing.

- "Leverage" (just say "use")
- "Utilize" (just say "use")
- "Facilitate" (just say "help" or "enable")
- "Synergy" / "synergize"
- "Actionable insights"
- "Move the needle"
- "Low-hanging fruit"
- "Circle back"
- "Paradigm shift"
- "Thought leadership"
- "Best-in-class"
- "Robust solution"
- "Scalable approach" (unless literally discussing system scaling)

## Performative Enthusiasm

Excitement that doesn't come from genuine conviction.

- "Amazing!" / "Incredible!" / "Game-changing!" without substantiation
- Exclamation marks on every other sentence
- "I'm SO excited to share..."
- "This blew my mind!"
- Adjective stacking: "This incredibly powerful and amazingly useful tool"

## Excessive Hedging

Undermining your own authority with unnecessary qualifiers.

- "I think maybe..." / "Perhaps it might be..."
- "In my humble opinion..."
- "I could be wrong, but..."
- "This is just my take, but..."
- Qualifying every single claim

## Important Nuance for Ben's Voice

Some phrases that are generally "AI slop" are things Ben uses authentically:
- **"Let's dive in!"**: Ben says this naturally. It's only slop when preceded by three paragraphs of throat-clearing.
- **Emoji**: Ben uses emoji genuinely and sparingly (😍, 🎉, 💀). It's slop when every paragraph has one.
- **"Simple!"**: Ben uses this as a one-word paragraph after showing something is, in fact, simple.
- **Lists**: Ben loves numbered lists. They're slop when every single section uses the exact same list format.
- **Exclamation marks**: Ben uses them for genuine enthusiasm ("Doesn't Phoenix and Elixir make this so so pretty? 😍"). They're slop when forced.

The test: does it feel like the writer *means it*, or like they're following a template?
